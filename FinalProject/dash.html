LATINO NETART PROGRAMMING
Each of the three net art pieces I've chosen encapsulates a fusion of net art elements, programming prowess, and a distinct touch of Latino influence. The first, map.jodi, is a dynamic database that weaves intricate connections through a network of links. It reminds me of a SQL and relational schemas class I'm taking this semester, which presents attributes and relationships, including one-to-one and many-to-one connections. The resulting data is then converted into a readable SQL file, which is then used in websites to streamline user, link, and post management. Moving on to the second website, mayhem we enter a captivating time capsule reminiscent of the early days of the internet. Users are immersed in an authentic web experience brimming with old school memes as they navigate through its links. However, the allure is tempered by the reality of broken links, a result of Flash technology's obsolescence. The third piece, in the end, captures the spirit of net art well. It exudes a classic charm and involves users in making decisions by showcasing art in a narrative cinematic style. Selecting several routes results in unique webpages, generating an individualised experience that embodies the fundamental principle of net art: enabling users to craft their own story and creative experience.
